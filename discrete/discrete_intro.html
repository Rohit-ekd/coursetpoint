Discrete Mathematics

Mathematics is broadly divided into two major branches -the discrete mathematics and continuous mathematics. Discrete mathematics mostly deals with only those real numbers which are multiple os some basic unit. If the basic unit is 1, then the discrete variable can assume only integral values. Discrete mathematics is a rapidly growing and increasingly used area of mathematics with many practical and relevant applications.

Mathematical Logic:

Logic is the science dealing with the methods of reasoning. Logic which which uses a symbolic language to express its principles in precise and unambiguous terms is known as mathematical logic. Mathematical logic has become an accepted part of discrete mathematics. One reason for this is that all efforts at the verification of algorithms inevitably the notation and methods of logic.

Boolean Algebra:

Binary logic deals with variables that take on two discrete values (like 1 or 0, True or False) and with operations that assume logical meaning. To build digital logic circuits of various families for different operations, the basic electronic circuits that are used are inverters and gates. Boolean Algebra is a simple and systematic way of representing and performing the operations of logic circuits in modern digital systems. The problem of optimizing the design of these circuits is essentially a problem of Boolean algebra.

Graph theory:

A graph is a set of points (commonly called vertices or nodes) in space that are interconnected by a set of lines (called edges). Graph theory is one of the most important branches of discrete mathematics and a source of many algorithms of practical importance. Among all the discrete structures, graphs are the most flexible and adaptable to suit the needs of applications. It has uses in areas such as syntactic analysis, fault detection and diagnosis in computers, and minimal path problems.

Relations & functions:

The concept of a relation is a basic concept in computer science as well as in real life. This concept arises when we consider a pair of objects and compare one another. A relational database stores information linking attributes of some set of individuals as a family of n-tuples for some n. Standard examples are database of airline booking or a medical histories and diagnoses of hospital patients.

One of the important concepts in discrete mathematics is that of function. The term "map", "mapping", "transformation", and many other mean the same thing. Relation in mathematics describe connections between different elements of the same set, whereas functions describe connections between two different sets.
