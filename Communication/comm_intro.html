Elements Of Communication
  • Source/Sender: Sender is the person who initiate the action of communication. Sender sends the message to the receiver.
  • Audience/Receiver: Receiver is the one or more person for whom the communication is intended.
  • Context/Environment: Refers to all condition surrounding the message and facilitating its proper decoding.
  • Medium/Channel:The channel serves as the medium through which the message is transmitted from the sender to the receiver.
  • Goal/PurposeGoal is the sender's reason for communication the desired result of the communication.
  • Message/ContentMessage is the information conveyed or which the sender want to gets the audience understood.
  • Feedback:Feedback is the receiver's response to the communication as observed by the sender.
Technical Documentation Presentation

Technical documentation presentation in communication is when information about technical subjects, like how to use software or build machinery, is organized and explained clearly. It involves using words, diagrams, and sometimes videos to help people understand complex ideas or processes. The goal is to make sure that anyone reading or watching can follow along easily and learn how to use or create something effectively.

Communication Skills

Communication skills refer to the ability to convey information effectively and clearly to others, whether verbally, in writing, or through non-verbal cues like body language. These skills are essential in various aspects of life, including personal relationships, work environments, and social interactions. Strong communication skills involve listening attentively, expressing ideas coherently, adapting communication style to different audiences, and being empathetic to others' perspectives. Effective communication fosters understanding, collaboration, and mutual respect, enabling individuals to convey their thoughts, feelings, and intentions accurately while also comprehending and responding to others' messages appropriately.

Principle of Business Communication

Business communication refers to the exchange of information within an organization or between organizations for the purpose of conducting business activities effectively. It encompasses various forms of communication channels, including verbal (such as face-to-face conversations, phone calls, and presentations), written (such as emails, memos, reports, and proposals), and non-verbal (such as body language and visual aids).

Written Communication

Written communication refers to the exchange of information using written words, symbols, or visual representations. It encompasses various forms of written communication, including emails, letters, memos, reports, proposals, manuals, and presentations.

Non-Verbal Communication

Non-verbal communication refers to the transmission of information and messages through means other than spoken or written words. It involves using body language, facial expressions, gestures, posture, tone of voice, eye contact, and other non-verbal cues to convey feelings, emotions, attitudes, and intentions.

Practical of Business Communication

Practical business communication refers to the effective exchange of information within a business setting, focusing on clear, efficient, and purposeful interactions to achieve specific objectives. It involves using various communication channels, such as emails, meetings, presentations, and reports, to convey messages, collaborate with colleagues, negotiate deals, and engage with clients or stakeholders. Practical business communication aims to streamline processes, foster understanding, and drive results in the context of organizational goals and objectives. It emphasizes clarity, professionalism, and adaptability to different audiences and situations, ultimately contributing to the success and productivity of the business.
