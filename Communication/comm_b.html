Concept & Imporants

The word "Communication" which comes from the latin word " Communicare" meaning to make common is used in common Talk,usually to mean speaking or writing or sending a message to another person communication is really much more than that.It involves ensuring that your message has reached the target audience (that is,the person to whom it is sent ) and that the receiver understand and respond as you want them to.It also involving ensuring that you yourself take care to receive, understand,interpret and respond to message that are sent to you. Communication is an important aspect of behaviour , human Communication is affected. by all factors that influence human behaviour.

Importances
  1. Sharing Of thoughts: The main Importance is that through communication sender can share his/her thoughts, views, idea etc. with receiver.
  2. Help in Decision Making: Communication helps in making Decision for future as well as presentation.
  3. Helping in Planning: Communication is that process which help to make formulate the plans by sender and Receiver discussion.
  4. Provide effective leadership: Communication is also help in effective leadership because leader could be lead their subordinate or employee through effective communication.
  5. Proper Coordination:Through effective leadership the impact of communication which Incurred in the mind of receiver or subordinate shows the impactful Coordination.
  6. Personal Development:Communication skills are vital for personal development and self-expression. They enable individuals to express their thoughts, feelings, and ideas confidently, assertively, and respectfully, leading to enhanced self-awareness and self-esteem.
  7. Cultural Understanding:Communication bridges cultural differences and promotes intercultural understanding and appreciation. It enables individuals from diverse backgrounds to interact, collaborate, and learn from one another, fostering inclusivity and diversity in society.
Process of Communication

The process of communication involves decision and activities by the two person involved the sender and the receiver. The sender starts this process.

  1. Sender: It is a person who want to send convey message,information,idea to the receiver. He/she is source and the one who starts the communication.
  2. Encoding: Message Generated by the sender is encodied symbolically such as in the form of word, pictures, Gestures etc. In other word the process of putting the idea into symbols is called encoding.
  3. Message: Once the Encoding is finished, the sender gets the message that he intends to convey. The message can be written, oral,symbolic or non-verbal body gestures, silence, sign etc. or any other signal that triggers the response of a reciever.
  4. Channel:The channel serves as the medium through which the message is transmitted from the sender to the receiver.
  5. ReceiverThe Receiver is the person for whom the message is intended or targated. Once the receiver receive the message and understand it, only then the process will be completed. The degree to which the reciever decodes the message depends on his knowledge of the subject matter, experience, trust and relation with the sender.
  6. DecodingDecoding is the process of translating the symbols into the ideas and interpreting the message is called decoding.
  7. Feedback:Feedback is the receiver's response to the communication as observed by the sender.
Types of Communication
Based on Channel
  1. Informal Communication:Informal Communication is that communication in which information are to be conveyed by the sender in unoffical way or unsystemmatic way that is also called Grapevine Communication.
  2. Formal Communication:The Communication which take place in a systematic or official way that is called formal communication. Basically this type of communication is used in Organization .
Based on Direction
  1. Upward Communication:The Communication which take place from bottom to top is called Upward Communication.
  2. Downward Communication:The Communication which take place from top to bottom is called Downward Communication.
  3. Horizontal Communication:The Communication which take place in it same level of an Organization that is called Horizontal Communication.
  4. Diagonal Communication:This is cross functional communication which take place between Employee at different level or department of an Organization.
Based on Method
  1. writing Communication:The Communication which take place through writen words for example: Application, Notice , Report etc.
  2. Oral Communication:The Communication in which Information which is flow with the help of words or through spoken words is called oral communication. for Example: Telephonic Communication.
Based on Expression
  1. Verbal Communication:It is one of the most common forms of communication and plays a central role in everyday interactions, professional settings, and social situations.
  2. Non-Verbal Communication:Person can communicate even without using any language this is known as non-verbal Communication. Example: face expression, body posture, gestures etc.
Barriers Of Communication

It refers to Unplanned distortion during the communication resulting in the receiver obtaining a different message than that sent by the sender.

Barriers in communication refer to obstacles or challenges that hinder the effective exchange of information between individuals or groups. These barriers can occur at any stage of the communication process and can prevent messages from being transmitted accurately or understood correctly. Barriers in communication can be categorized into several types:

Physical Barriers

These are tangible obstacles that affect communication, such as distance, noise, poor lighting, or physical barriers like walls or partitions that make it difficult for individuals to see or hear each other.

  1. Defects in Medium
  2. Noise in the Environment
  3. Information Overload
Semantic or Language Barriers

Semantic barriers arise from differences in language, vocabulary, or understanding of terms. These barriers can occur due to jargon, technical language, or cultural differences, leading to misunderstandings or misinterpretations of messages.

  1. Faulty Translation
  2. Specilist Language
  3. Cultural Difference
Psychological Barriers

Psychological factors such as misperception, filtering, distrust, Unhappy emotions and people's of mind can jeopardize the process of Communication.

  1. Difference in Perception
  2. Prejudices
  3. Information Overload
Cultural Barriers

Cultural barriers result from differences in cultural norms, values, beliefs, or communication styles between individuals or groups from different cultural backgrounds.

  1. Values and Norms of Behavior
  2. Language
  3. Social Relationship
  4. Concept of time
Organizational Barriers

Organizational barriers arise from factors within the organizational structure, such as hierarchy, bureaucracy, poor leadership, or lack of communication channels or policies. These barriers can inhibit the flow of information, hinder feedback, or create silos within the organization.

  1. Status Relationship
  2. One Way flow
  3. Organization Structure
  4. Organization policy
Technical Barriers

Technological barriers occur when issues with communication tools or technology disrupt the transmission or reception of messages. This can include problems with internet connectivity, malfunctioning equipment, or unfamiliarity with communication software or platforms.

  1. Internet Connectivity
  2. Lack of Technical knowledge
  3. Failure of equipment
Social Barriers

Social barriers in communication refer to obstacles or challenges that arise from social factors, norms, or dynamics within a particular social context, which can hinder effective communication between individuals or groups.

  1. Social Status
  2. Prejudice and Discrimination
  3. Gender Dynamics
  4. Interpersonal Relationships
