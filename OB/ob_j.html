Power & Politics

Power: "Power is the capacity of a person, team or organisation to influence others." For Example : A capacity that A has to influence the behaviour of B so that B acts in accordance with A's wishes.

Politics : "Politics are the activities managers engage to increase their power and use it to achieve thier goals."

Source of Power
  1. Formal Power
    • Coercive power(punishment power) : Power that is based on fear. It is the ability to administer unpleasent conquences. The implied force relates to threats and punishment.
    • Reward Power : Compliance achieved based on the ability to distrube rewards that others view as valuable.
    • Legitimate Power : The power a person receives as a result of his or her position in the formal hierarchy of an organisation.
    • Information Power : Power that comes from access to and control over information.
    Personal Power
    • Expert Power : Influence based on special skills or knowledge. Followers must perceive the agent as an expert, and trust that the expertise is valid and that the knowledge or skills are relevant and useful to them.
    • Referent Power : Influence based on possession by an individual or desirable resources or personal traits. It is based on interpersonal attraction.
    • Charismatic Power : An extension of referent power stemming from an individual's personality and interpersonal style.

Politics Tactics & Strategies

To make effective use of organisational politics, leader must be aware to specific political tactics & strategies. There are three major tactics

  • Gaining Power : All political tactics are aimed at gaining & maintaining power, even the power to avoid a difficult assignment.
    • Developed power contacts
    • control vital information
    • control lines of communication
    • Bring in outside expert
    • Make a quick showing
  • Building Relationship : Much of organisation politics involve building relationship with network member who can be helpful now or later.
    • Make & manage impression on others
    • Ask satisfied customers to speak about you
    • Be overly courteous, pleasant & positive
    • Flatter others
  • Avoiding political Blunders : A strategy for retaining power is to refrain from making power eroding blunders which can pervent one from attaining power.
    • Critizing the boss in public forums
    • By passing the manager
    • Overt display of disloyalty
    • Being or perceived as a poor team player
    • Act in haste
    • Declining an offer from top management
Conflict Management
  1. Conflict management is the practice of being able to identify and handle conflict sensibly, fairly, and efficiently. since conflicts in a business are a natural part of workplace.
  2. It is important that there are people who understand conflicts and know how to resolve them. This is important in today's market more than ever.
  3. Unmanaged or poorly managed conflicts generate a breakdown in trust and lost productivity.
Conflict Management Strategies
  1. Avoiding : The desire to withdraw from or suppress a conflict.
  2. Accommodating : The willingness of one party in a conflict to place the opponent's interests above his or her own.
  3. Compromising : A situation in which each party to a conflict is willing to give up something.
  4. Competing : A desire to satisfy one's interests, regardless of the impact on other party to the conflict.
  5. Collaborating : A situation in which the parties to a conflict each desire to satisfy fully the concerns of all parties.
Steps to Conflict Resolution
  1. Cost Effective Atmosphere
  2. Clarify Perceptions
  3. Focus on individual and shared needs
  4. Build shared positive power
  5. Look to the future and learn from the past
  6. Generate Options
  7. Develop "Do-ables"
  8. Make Mutual Benefit Agreements
