Personality

The word personality is derived from a latin word "persona" which means "to speak through." Personality is the combination of characteristics or qualities that forms a person's unique idenity. It signifies the role which a person plays in public.

Determinants of Personality
  1. Biological factors :
    • Brain/Mental factor : Brain is one of the most important factors of personality determinant. It is generally believed that the father and the child adopt almost the same type of brain stimulation and the later differences are the result of the enivronment in which the child has been grown up.
    • Physical factor : One of the most important factors in determining personality is the 'physical characteristics' of an individual. It is believed that this factors plays a vital role in determining one's behaviour in any organisation.
    • Heredity factor : Heredity factor are the ones that are determined at the time of conception. These factors are not only affect the physical features of a person, but the intelligence level, attentiveness, gender, temperament, various inherited diseases and energy level, all get affected by them.
  2. Social Factor: Social factors also play a vital role in determining one's personality. The society that we live in, the cultural environment that we face daily, the community we get interacted to, all are included in this factor.
  3. Cultural & Religious Factor : The cultural factor in which one lives in that may involve traditional practices, norms, customs, procedures, rule and regulations, precedents and values, all the important determinants of personality.
  4. Situational Factor : Althrough these factors do not literally create and shape up an individual's personality, situational factors do alter a person's behavior and response from time to time. The situational factors can be commonly observed when a person behaves contrastingly and exhibits different traits and characteristics.
  5. Family Factor : The Family has very powerful affects on the developing child, impacting attitudes, beliefs, opportunities, habits and personality traits.
Theory of Personality
  1. Psychoanalytical Theory/Psychological Theory (Sigmund Freud)
    "The Basic notion on which Psychoanalytical theory is base on is that human behaviour is influenced more by unseen forces than conscious and rational thoughts."
    Freud Theory is based on the concept
    1. Id : Id is the original and the most basic system of human personality. It consists of everything psychologically that is inherited and present at the time of birth.
    2. Ego : As the Individual learns to seprate the unreality from reality in childhood, the ego develops. The ego is reality oriented part in thinking; it is largely practical and works in an executive capacity. Ego is associated with the realities of life.
    3. Super Ego : The super Ego is the higher level force to restrain the Id and is described as the conscious of a person. The super ego represents the norms of the individual, his family and the society and is an ethical constraint on the behaviour.
  2. Socio-Psychoanalytical Theory (Neo-Freud)
    It asserts that individuals and society are interlinked. This mean on individual strives to meet the needs of the social and the society help him to attain his goals. Through this interaction the personality of an individual at determines.
  3. Trait Theory (Gordon allport)
    It asserts that an individual is composed of a set of definite predisposition attributes called as traits. There traits are distinguishment and often long lasting quality or characteristic of a person that makes him different from other.
    "Trait mean those special qualities of an individual person which makes him different from others."
    O.C.E.A.N (Openness , Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neutroticism)
    • Openness : Openness(also refered to as openness to experience). People who are high in this personality trait also tend to be more adventurous and creative.
    • Conscientiousness : Among each of the personality traits, conscientiousness is one defined by high levels of thoughtfulness, good impluse control, goal-directed behaviours.
    • Extraversion : Extraversion(or extroversion) is a personality trait characterised by excitiability, sociability, talkactiveness, assertiveness and high amounts of emotional expressiveness. Being around others helps them feel energized and excited.
    • Agreeableness : This personality trait includes attributes such as trust, kindness, affection and other prosocial behaviours.
    • Neutroticism : Neutroticism is characterised by sadness, moodiness and emotional instability individuals, who are high in Neutroticism tend to experience mood swings and sadness.
  4. Self Theory (Carl Rogers)
    It Emphasizes on the set of perception an individual has or himself and the perception of the relationship he was with other and the other aspects of life.

    Factors of Self Theory:

    • Self Image : By Nature, every person has certain beliefs about what or who he/she is. b.Ideal self: The Ideal self refers to what one would like to do, what one would like to be.
    • Ideal self: The Ideal self refers to what one would like to do, what one would like to be.
    • Looking Glass self : The looking glass self is the perception of a person about how others are preceiving his qualities and characteristics.
    • Real self : The Real self is what one really is.
Personality attributes Influence by OB

There are several personality attributes:

  1. Locus of Control :It is degree to which individual believe they are masters of their own fate. Based on this attribute, people can be classified as:
    • Internals : Individual who believe that they are masters of their own fate. They control what happens to them. They generally perform better on the job.
    • Externals : Individual who believe that their fate is controlled by outside force. It can be chance or luck. They are less satisfied the job.
  2. Machiavellianism : : The trait aims to gain power and control over others. It is the degree to which an Individual in pragmatic, maintain emotional distance and belives that ends can justify means "If it works, use it."
  3. Self Esteem : It is Individual's degree of liking or disliking of themselves. It is belief about one's own worth. It is the way one feels about oneself.
  4. Authoritarianism: The concept refers to belief that there should be status and power differences among people in Organisations.
  5. Self Monitoring : Self monitoring is a personality trait that measures an individual's ability to adjust his or her behaviour to external situational factor.
  6. Risk Taking : The propensity of people to assume risk or avoids risks varies from person to person depending upon the willingness of the people to take chances. This human trait will affect the decision making capability of a manager.
  7. 'Type A' and 'Type B' Personsility : People how are impatient aggressive and highly competitive and termed as 'type A' personality. but those who are easy going, laid pack and non-competitive are termed as 'type B' personality.
  8. Introversion and Extroversion : These two terms are generally associated with the interpersonal behaviour of an individual and his sociability. Extroverts are gregarious and sociable individual while introverts are shy, quite and retiring.
