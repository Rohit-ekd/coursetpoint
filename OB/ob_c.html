Concept of Attitude

An Attitude can be defined as a predisposition to response in a favorable or unfavorable way to objects or persons in one's environment. When we dislikes or likes something, we are in effect, Expressing our attitude towards the person or object.

Attitude is very complex coginitive process just like personality of an individual.

    There are Two types of attitude

  • Positive Attitude : Positive Mental attitude is a psychological term which describes a mental phenomenon is which the central idea is that one can increase achievement through optimistic thought process.
  • Negative Attitude : A Negative attitude is characteristized by a great disdain for everything. Someone who constantly points the negative in everthing.
Feature of Attitude
  1. Attitude will change with time and situation.
  2. Related to feeling and beliefs of people.
  3. Effect one's behaviours positively or negatively.
  4. Affect perception.
  5. Learned through experiences.
  6. May be unconsciously held.
Nature of Attitude
  1. Aspects of Valence: The degree of favorableness or unfavorableness towards the event.
  2. Aspects of multiplexity :Number of element constituting the attitude.
  3. Relation to need aspects : Vary in relation to needs they serve.
  4. Centrality aspect : Importance of attitude object to someone.
Process of Attitude
  1. Cognitive : The opinion or belief segment of an attitude based on the knowledge or information.
  2. Affective : The emotional or feeling segment of an attitude.
  3. Behavioural : An intention to behave in acertain way to words someone or something.
Formation of Attitude
  1. Experience with object : Attitude can develop from a personally rewarding or punishing experience with object.
  2. Classical conditioning : It involves involuntary responses and is acquried through thepairing of two stimuli.
  3. Operant conditioning : It is based on the"law of effect" and involves voluntary responses, behaviors.
  4. Vicarious learning : Formation of attitude by observing behaviour of others and consequences of that behaviour.
  5. Family and Peer groups : A person may learn attitude through imitation of parents.
    • Neighbourhood : Involves being told what attitudes to have by parents, school, community organization, religious doctrine, friends.
    • Economics status : Our economical and occuptional position also contribute to attitude formation.
  6. Mass Communication : Tv, radio, newspaper and magazine feed their audiences large quantities of information.
